Privacy Policy

ShoppingList app — Last updated: July 2026

Who develops this app?

ShoppingList is developed and maintained by an independent developer, on a personal basis (not a company or commercial entity).

What data is collected?

None. ShoppingList does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data with anyone. The app contains no analytics tools, no advertising SDKs, and no third-party tracking services.

Where is my data (lists, items, categories) stored?

All the data you create in the app (shopping lists, items, categories, preferences) is stored exclusively locally on your device, in a private database used only by the app. Nothing is sent to any external server.

Does the app use the Internet?

Not in its current version. Some features planned for future versions (for example, product lookup via the Open Food Facts database for barcode scanning) will require an Internet connection to query that third-party service. When that happens, this policy will be updated before the feature is released to clearly explain what information is involved and which service it is sent to.

Permissions requested

The app does not request any sensitive permission (no access to your contacts, location, camera, or personal storage) in its current version. Any future permission added (for example, camera access for barcode scanning) will be clearly explained in an update to this page.

Deleting your data

Since all data stays only on your device, simply uninstalling the app or clearing its cache/data from your Android settings will permanently delete all associated information.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated if new features involve different data handling (Google sign-in, online sync, etc.). The "last updated" date at the top of this page always reflects the most recent version.
